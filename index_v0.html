About Me

Mission Log: System Initialization Complete. Greetings Traveller! Welcome to the build bay. I’m a robotics grad student who lives at the intersection of hardware, software, and controlled chaos. My core loop? Understand systems at the lowest level possible, then make them move intelligently in the real (or simulated) world. From designing motion planners and tuning control loops to wiring up harnesses, sensors, and autonomous stacks, I enjoy turning complex systems into something that just works. I’ve built and tested autonomous racing simulators, wrangled ROS 2 pipelines, trained reinforcement learning agents, and designed race-ready hardware—because great autonomy doesn’t live in software alone. Think less “black box AI,” more Iron Man in the garage: iterate, test, break it, fix it, and ship something better. Outside mission time, I recharge through anime marathons, swimming laps, parkour sessions, and the occasional side quest in graphic design. Still optimizing the human system—firmware updates ongoing. Status: Curious. Builder-minded. Always ready for the next challenge.
